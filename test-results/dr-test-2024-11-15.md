## what changed since test 1

The backup NAS mount issue was the only major blocker in test 1. Once that was fixed, the actual restore time was similar — pg_restore took about 1h 15m in both tests (the October test's extended time was from investigation, not restore). WAL replay also took about 90 minutes in both.

The `pg_is_in_recovery()` check in the runbook caught the standby mode issue — after WAL replay, postgres was waiting for a promote signal and the app couldn't write. The runbook now includes `pg_ctl promote` as an explicit step after WAL replay.

## observations and notes

The WAL replay duration (90 minutes) is something to watch. This is eating into the database RTO budget. With 3h 48m total, there's only 12 minutes of headroom. If the database grows or WAL accumulation increases, we'll miss target again. Options to investigate: reduce checkpoint intervals to create more frequent checkpoints (less WAL to replay), or move to streaming replication to site-b so we don't need WAL replay at all.

The network and web tier are well within targets. Those phases are essentially solved.
